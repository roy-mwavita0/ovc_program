CPARA, Case Plan & Services Layering

Case plan layering, in simple terms, is a case management process of addressing the needs of Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC) and their households by identifying gaps across various domains—healthy, stable, safe, and schooled—and providing interventions accordingly.

Case plan layering ensures that services provided are tracked and evaluated against the identified needs.

Below is a comprehensive visual representation showcasing:

- All identified gaps within the CPARA

- Specific needs developed for each missing benchmark

- Services provided to address those needs for each benchmark
